# Slow internet

- Ask whether the issue happens on Wi‑Fi, mobile data, or both.
- Ask the customer to restart the router or phone and retry.
- Check for any known network outages and tell the customer the expected ETA.
- If it still persists, escalate to technical support for deeper troubleshooting.

# Change mobile plan

- Verify the customer's identity and current plan.
- Briefly explain available plans, price, and any contract impacts.
- Offer to process the plan change now and explain when it will take effect.
- Send a confirmation message with new plan details and any charges.

# No signal

- Ask whether the problem is indoors or in a specific area and if others are affected.
- Suggest toggling airplane mode or restarting the phone, then recheck signal.
- Check for known coverage or maintenance issues and inform the customer.
- If unresolved, arrange further network checks or advise visiting a service center.

# SIM not working after phone change

- Confirm the SIM is correctly inserted and the phone model is compatible.
- Ask the customer to restart the phone and test calls/data.
- Guide through activating or re-provisioning the SIM if needed.
- If still not working, offer to arrange a SIM replacement or remote support.

# Can’t receive OTP messages

- Verify the phone number on file and confirm SMS is not blocked on the device.
- Ask the customer to check the message inbox, spam folder, and messaging app settings.
- Suggest restarting the phone and testing with a normal SMS from another number.
- If OTPs still fail, offer alternative verification options or escalate to technical support.

# Late bill payment

- Verify the customer's account and confirm the outstanding balance.
- Explain the due date, any late fees or interest charges, and payment options (online, phone, in-store).
- Reassure them that paying now will prevent service suspension or credit impact.
- Offer a one-time courtesy waiver if applicable based on account history.
- Send a payment confirmation and updated balance after processing.

# Upgrade mobile plan

- Ask the customer what features or data they need (more data, international roaming, etc.).
- Show available upgrade options, pricing, and how it affects their contract.
- Confirm whether they want to keep their current number and existing services.
- Apply the upgrade immediately and send a confirmation with the new plan details.
- Explain when the new charges will appear on their next bill.

# Cancel broadband service

- Confirm the customer's identity and the reason for cancellation.
- Explain any early termination fees, final bill timing, and data backup recommendations.
- Ask if they would consider a downgrade to a lower plan instead.
- Schedule the service disconnection date and confirm delivery of any equipment to return.
- Provide a cancellation number and instructions for equipment return.

# Disputed bill or unexpected charges

- Listen to the customer's concern and locate the specific charge on their bill.
- Explain what the charge is for (usage-based, service fee, promotion reversal, etc.).
- Check if it is a duplicate, billing error, or unauthorized service.
- If valid, clarify the terms; if erroneous, process a credit immediately.
- If contested, escalate to billing for dispute investigation and follow-up within 5–7 days.

# Lost or stolen phone

- Ask the customer to describe the phone (model, color, IMEI) and when/where it was lost.
- Advise them to contact the phone manufacturer or carrier's device tracking service immediately.
- Recommend blocking the SIM to prevent unauthorized use and charges.
- Explain device insurance options and replacement process if available.
- Discuss re-provisioning their old SIM to a backup device or ordering a replacement.

# SIM card not working

- Ask the customer if the SIM was recently changed, inserted, or replaced.
- Guide them to check that the SIM is correctly aligned and fully inserted.
- Suggest restarting the device and checking signal/data after 1–2 minutes.
- Ask if they see any error messages ("No SIM" or "Invalid SIM").
- If still unresolved, offer a replacement SIM kit or escalate to technical support for remote diagnostics.
